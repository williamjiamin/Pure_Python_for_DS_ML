# Chapter 80: Diffusion Models (DDPM) from Scratch in Pure Python

## Why this chapter matters

## DDPM recap
Forward process gradually adds Gaussian noise:

`q(x_t | x_{t-1}) = N(sqrt(alpha_t) x_{t-1}, beta_t I)`

Closed form:

`x_t = sqrt(alpha_bar_t) x_0 + sqrt(1 - alpha_bar_t) epsilon`

where `alpha_t = 1 - beta_t`, `alpha_bar_t = prod_{s=1..t} alpha_s`.

Model learns to predict `epsilon` from `(x_t, t)`.

Reverse mean estimate:

`mu_theta(x_t, t) = 1/sqrt(alpha_t) * (x_t - beta_t/sqrt(1-alpha_bar_t) * epsilon_theta(x_t,t))`

Sampling starts from noise `x_T ~ N(0, I)` and iterates backward.

## Simplification used here
To keep implementation pure Python and transparent:
- 1D data distribution (mixture of Gaussians)
- per-timestep linear denoiser: `eps_hat = a_t * x_t + b_t`
- SGD training over random `(x0, t, eps)` triples

This is not SOTA architecture, but it reproduces DDPM mechanics clearly.
